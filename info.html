Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

Discover the beauty of one of Karnataka's most famous bird sanctuaries, a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Quick Facts

Coordinates: 12°24'N 76°39'E

Location: Mandya, Karnataka, India

Area: 40 acres (16 ha)

Established: 1940

Visitors: 304,000 (2016-17)

Governing Body: Ministry of Environment and Forests, Government of India

History

The islets in the Kaveri river were formed when an embankment was built between 1645 and 1648. The area was later declared a protected sanctuary in 1940. Ornithologist Salim Ali helped ensure its protection, and it became a haven for various bird species.

Flooding

During certain rainy seasons, the sanctuary experiences heavy flooding. Water released from the Krishna Raja Sagara dam can lead to temporary suspension of boating activities.

Biome

The sanctuary primarily lies within a riparian zone, providing a perfect habitat for birds and other wildlife.

Flora

Riverine reed beds cover the banks, with broadleaf forests and species like Terminalia arjuna (Arjun tree) and Pandanus trees. The endemic lily, Iphigenia mysorensis, is also found here.

Fauna

Birds

Over 170 bird species can be spotted, including painted storks, pelicans, and migratory birds like Siberian ducks. The sanctuary is especially popular during the nesting season.

Mammals and Reptiles

The sanctuary is home to small mammals like the bonnet macaque and smooth-coated otter, along with reptiles like the mugger crocodile and monitor lizard.

Activities

Visitors can enjoy ranger-guided boat tours, which offer opportunities to spot birds, crocodiles, otters, and bats. The best time to visit is during the nesting season (June–November) and to see migratory birds (December–February).

Accessibility

Nearest Town: Srirangapatna (3 km), Nearest City: Mysuru (19 km), Nearest Airport: Mysore Airport (25 km).
